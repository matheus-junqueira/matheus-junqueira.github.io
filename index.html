I am a Predoctoral Fellow at the Global Research Training Initiative (GRTI-UPPER) at Johns Hopkins SAIS, working with Professors Filipe Campante and Shoumitro Chatterjee. My research sits at the intersection of political economy, international trade, and applied econometrics, with a focus on how political institutions shape economic outcomes.

I hold an M.A. in Economics from FGV-EESP (2025) and a B.A. in Economics from FEA-USP (2021). My master's thesis used a regression discontinuity design across 1,064 national elections (1945–2018) to identify the causal effect of left-wing electoral victories on trade policy.

Working Paper · September 2025

From Ballots to Borders

The Influence of Political Regimes on Global Trade Policies

Are left-wing governments protectionist? Using a regression discontinuity design across 1,064 national elections (1945–2018), this paper shows that left-wing administrations adopt significantly more protectionist measures — raising import tariffs and reducing import volumes while promoting exports. Effects vary meaningfully across presidential, parliamentary, and semi-presidential systems.
